The blue liquid is cytoplasm.

The orange outside of the cell is the cell membrane.

The purple inside of the cell is the cytoskeleton.

The light pink envelope around the nucleus is the nuclear membrane.

The black dots inside of the cell are the ribosomes. The free ribosomes are suspended in the cytoplasm, and the bound ribosomes are on the Rough ER.


Here is a table with all of the organelles' functions:

OrganelleFunction
RibosomeCreates proteins
Rough ERTransportation of proteins to Golgi Apparatus
Smooth ERLipid Synthesis, Manufacturing
Golgi ApparatusCreates complex molecules
NucleusContains DNA (Instructions)
Nuclear MembraneProtects DNA
LysosomeBreaks down waste
MitochondriaBreaks down nutrients. Powerhouse of the cell.
CytoskeletonHelps cell keep it's shape
VacuoleStores things
CytoplasmKeeps organelles in place
